For ex-situ evaluation of plasma synthesized nanomaterials, LPN-PPPL scientists use Raman spectroscopy, Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM), Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM), Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM), and BET measurements for surface area determination in the Princeton Institute for the Science and Technology of Materials (PRISM) and by X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS) and other surface analytical techniques in Koel’s labs at PPPL and at Princeton University (Koel's lab at Princeton University).


Secondary Electron Microscope

The center has a FEI Quanta environmental SEM that allows imaging of insulator materials with minimal sample preparation. The unit is equipped with an Oxford energy dispersive spectrometer that allows chemical analysis of materials at nanoscale.
More details at PRISM Imaging and Analysis Center.

Transmission Electron Microscope

High resolution imaging of nanomaterials is carried out by a Philip CM200 field emission TEM at 200 kV. In addition to the bright field imaging, the unit also provides selected area electron diffraction (SAED) that probes structural properties of nanomaterials.
More details at PRISM Imaging and Analysis Center.


X-Ray Diffractometer

In addition to the conventional θ-2θ configuration, the Bruker Discover D8 unit is able to operate in grazing angle diffraction, in which the structural information of thin film as thin as few tens of nanometers can be studied. The unit utilizes Cu Kα as the x-ray source.
More details at PRISM Imaging and Analysis Center.

Micro-Raman Spectroscopy

The Horiba Raman spectrometer is coupled to objective lenses from 10x to 100x, which provides high spatial selectivity with minimal sample preparation. The unit is equipped with four probing wavelengths at 325 nm, 532 nm, 633 nm, and 785 nm. The UV source is especially useful to study wide band gap materials such as boron nitride and nanocrystalline diamond.
More details at PRISM Imaging and Analysis Center.




Atomic Force Microscopy

The Bruker Dimension AFM allows one to carry out metrology of nanomaterials.
More details at PRISM Imaging and Analysis Center.
